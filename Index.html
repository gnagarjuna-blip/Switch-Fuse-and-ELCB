Protection Devices

Question: Explain about i) Switch Fuse Unit (SFU) and ii) ELCB with neat sketches.

Introduction:
i) Switch Fuse Unit (SFU): It is a combination of a switch and a fuse unit used for manual switching and protection of electrical circuits against overcurrent and short circuits.

ii) Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker (ELCB): It is a safety device used in electrical installations with high Earth impedance to prevent shock by detecting small stray voltages on the metal enclosures of electrical equipment.
Main Components:
For SFU:
* Enclosure (Box)
* Operating Handle
* Moving Contacts & Fixed Contacts
* HRC (High Rupturing Capacity) Fuses

For ELCB:
* Trip Coil
* Test Button
* Current Sensing Transformer (in Voltage-ELCB)
Working Mechanism:
SFU: When the handle is turned 'ON', the contacts close and power flows through the fuse. If current exceeds the fuse rating, the fuse wire melts, breaking the circuit.

ELCB: It monitors the balance of current between the Phase and Neutral. If a leakage occurs (current flows to Earth), the balance is disturbed, creating a magnetic field that triggers the trip coil to open the circuit.
i) Switch Fuse Unit (SFU):
The SFU is primarily used for low-voltage applications. It provides a means of manual isolation (the switch) and automatic protection (the fuse). It is widely used in industrial distribution boards for motor feeders and main incomers.

ii) Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker (ELCB):
The primary purpose of an ELCB is to detect "Earth Leakage." There are two types:
  • Voltage ELCB: Detects a rise in potential on the equipment body.
  • Current ELCB (RCCB): Detects the difference between outgoing and incoming current.
If a person touches a faulty appliance, the leakage current is detected within milliseconds (usually 30mA), and the ELCB trips the power to save lives.
